Reconnecting With Inspiration

Posted on March 25, 2022 by Scott French

The last entry ended at a bit of a low spot with me questioning where I fit into the guitar world and not finding a satisfactory answer before I had to return to software developent to pay the bills. It's been many years so I've had a lot of time figure it out. It was only recently that the idea was really cemented in my head by observing how other creative people found their place.

Going back to the very beginging of my instrument building journey, with the initial creation of my main designs and aesthetic, I found myself often making choices based on eliminating options. I think in any creative endevor reducing your options can be a great exercise. I often used this technique for making choices by removing options until eventually I hit a point where my instruments were somewhat recognizable. I had ened up with some unique features only after dedicating myself to avoiding common options for various reasons.

Over time I have applied this same technique toward deciding where I fit into the guitar building world. Right away I can cut off the very top. I know I am not gunning to be the next Steve Klein or Ken Parker building guitars that cost as much as a new family sedan. These guys are heros for sure, but I know myself and how much time I have available, I'm not going to earn those kind of chops overnight. As much as I love those insane archtops and acoustics, it's not the world I want to be operating in. I can also cut off the bottom and mid tiers. I have no interest in outsourcing my designs to the east so that pretty much ends that discussion. The level of complexity and quality of materials I want to use means I will be operating above the mid-tier but below the absolute pinacle of high-end builders.

I wish that was more of a revalation, but that is basically the place where most small shop instruments operate. I had actually wasted a lot of time trying to come up with plans to compete at the mid-tier but reducing complexity and buying in bulk, but I wasn't able to make it work. I had also spent some time working with the idea of outsourcing my design, but my heart was never in it, I knew I wanted to avoid exploiting cheap labor.
